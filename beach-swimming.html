Golden Bay beach guide

Beach days, done properly.

If you are staying in Pōhara, you are already close to one of Golden Bay’s easiest family swims. From there, the smart move is choosing the right beach for the day, not just the nearest one. Ligar Bay is warmer and gentler for little kids, Tata is better for deeper swimming, and Tōtaranui is the scenic full-day version.

Where to start

Pōhara is the anchor beach.

Pōhara is still the smartest default. Nelson Tasman visitor guidance describes it as a vast golden beach that draws swimmers, kayakers and boaties, with lots of room at low tide and shade from the vegetation edge. If you want one beach that works for a casual swim, a paddleboard, and a barefoot walk in the same afternoon, this is it.

Ligar Bay Warmest family feel

Ligar Bay is the beach to choose when younger kids are the centre of the plan. Local guides consistently describe it as sheltered, safe at high tide, and especially good at low tide when the pools and western-end rocks become part of the fun.

Tata Beach Deeper swim

Tata is the more flexible swim if you do not want to time the tide as tightly. Golden Bay guides note that it is deeper for swimming through the day, while Nelson Tasman promotes it as a sheltered base for swimming, kayaking, and boating.

Tōtaranui is the scenic upgrade

If you want a bigger outing, Tōtaranui is the one to plan. It is not the casual pop-in option, but it gives you the full golden-sand, Abel Tasman version of a beach day, with swimming, kayaking, and bush walks all in reach.

Supplied beach image, Tōtaranui Beach

Not every famous beach is a swimming beach

Wharariki is unforgettable, but it is not the place for a casual swim. Strong currents and wild surf make it better as a dramatic walk, dune day, or seal-viewing stop than a swimming pick.

Choose by mood

Pick the beach that matches the day.

The best beach in Golden Bay depends on who is with you, what the tide is doing, and whether you want a quick swim or a whole outing. This is the short version.

For younger kids

Ligar Bay is usually the strongest answer.

  • Warmer shallows and protected feel
  • Rock pools and exploring at low tide
  • Peaceful setting with less water-sport energy

For a proper swim

Tata Beach is the better call when you want deeper water.

  • More forgiving through the day
  • Good if you do not want to chase one tide window
  • Works well with kayaks and summer activity

For easy convenience

Pōhara wins when the goal is simple and close.

  • Closest to accommodation and cafés
  • Great default swim without planning much
  • Easy to combine with a walk or paddle
Practical read

What actually helps on the day.

The difference between a good beach decision and a flat one is usually tide, age range, and how much time you want to spend in the car.

Quick rules

  • Choose Pōhara when you want the easiest beach day from your base.
  • Choose Ligar Bay when toddlers or younger kids are setting the pace.
  • Choose Tata when swimming matters more than low-tide exploring.
  • Choose Tōtaranui when the beach is the outing, not just one stop in it.
  • Choose Whariwharangi when you want the quiet, remote bay version of an Abel Tasman beach day.

Simple cautions

  • High tide generally gives the cleanest, easiest swim at Pōhara and Ligar.
  • Ligar is still great at low tide, just in a different way, more splash and explore than swim laps.
  • Tōtaranui is worth going for, but treat it like a planned day out.
  • Save Wharariki for scenery, not swimming.
