Beach & Swimming

Golden Bay has some of the best family swimming beaches in the top of the South Island. Base yourself in Pōhara, then explore nearby favourites like Ligar Bay, Tata Beach, and Tōtaranui depending on the tide, the kids, and how adventurous you feel.

Local Favourite

Start with Pōhara Beach

Pōhara is the easy default if you want a classic Golden Bay beach day without overthinking it. Official Nelson Tasman visitor info describes it as a vast golden beach that draws swimmers, kayakers and boaties, with plenty of space at low tide and vegetation-fringed areas for shade. It is the most convenient option if you are staying in Pōhara and want swimming, paddleboarding, or a long barefoot walk all in one place.

Best Beaches

Where to swim around Golden Bay

Each beach has a slightly different feel. These are the standouts if swimming is high on the agenda.

Pōhara Beach

Best for convenience, space, and an easy all-round beach day. Golden Bay tourism sources describe Pōhara as a shallow tidal beach with soft sand and warm high-tide swims, making it especially appealing for families and relaxed summer afternoons.

Ligar Bay

Best for younger children and warm shallows. Local destination guides describe Ligar Bay as largely sheltered, safe for swimming at high tide, and ideal for kids at low tide thanks to warm tidal pools, rock exploration, and a peaceful no-boating feel.

Tata Beach

Best for deeper swimming and a brighter, more classic postcard beach feel. Golden Bay beach guides note that Tata is deeper for swimming at any time of day, while Nelson Tasman promotes it as a sheltered launching point for swimming, kayaking, and boating adventures.

Worth the Drive

Tōtaranui for the full golden-sand experience

If you want a bigger outing, head to Tōtaranui at the northern end of Abel Tasman National Park. Tourism and DOC sources both describe it as a long golden-sand beach that is great for relaxing, swimming, kayaking, and pairing with native bush walks. It is less of a quick dip beach and more of a full-day mission, but the setting is beautiful.

Swimming

For a simple swim close to home, start with high tide at Pōhara. If you want warmer shallows and easy splash zones for small kids, Ligar Bay is usually the stronger pick. If you prefer deeper water and don’t want to time the tide as carefully, Tata Beach is the better option.

Paddleboarding & Kayaking

Pōhara and Tata are the most natural choices for paddleboards and kayaks. Pōhara gives you plenty of room and easy access, while Tata’s sheltered bay and island-dotted outlook make it especially good for a more scenic on-water session.

Families

If you are travelling with younger children, Ligar Bay deserves a special mention. Its sheltered inlet, tidal pools, rock-hopping, and calmer feel make it one of the best family beaches in Golden Bay for a slower, easier day.

Good to Know

Tips before you go

  • Pōhara is best if you want an easy beach day with cafés and accommodation close by.
  • Ligar Bay shines at high tide for swimming and at low tide for kids exploring pools and rocks.
  • Tata Beach works well when you want deeper water and more flexibility through the day.
  • Tōtaranui is worth planning as a longer outing rather than a quick pop-in swim.
  • Wharariki is spectacular, but it is not considered a safe swimming beach because of strong currents and wild surf.
Staying in Pōhara

Why this page starts here

If you are staying in Pōhara, you are already on one of Golden Bay’s best swimming beaches. The real advantage is that you can enjoy an easy local swim, then choose whether to branch out to Ligar Bay, Tata, or Tōtaranui based on the day’s weather, the tide, and who is in the car.
