Golden Bay nature guide

Wildlife, right at your door.

From a Pōhara base, you do not have to go far to see seals, dolphins, and forest birdlife. If you are looking for Golden Bay wildlife experiences, Cape Farewell gives you the easiest seal viewing, the bay itself can deliver dolphin encounters, and the reserves around Tākaka and the Abel Tasman edge round out the day with strong native birdlife.

Where to watch

Cape Farewell is the anchor spot.

Just minutes from Pōhara, Cape Farewell is the most reliable place to see seals in Golden Bay. The colony is present year-round, and the viewing is straightforward, walk to the headland, look down, and you will usually see them on the rocks below. It is also easy to fold into a Wharariki stop or a slower scenic loop across the top of the bay.

Dolphin encounters Seasonal

Common dolphins are regular visitors to Golden Bay from December through March. They are curious and often approach boats and kayaks. Winter sightings are less common but do happen.

Boat-based wildlife Best odds

Charter boats and kayak tours from Pōhara give you the best chance of dolphin encounters. The operators know the patterns and can often find them when they are in the bay.

Birdlife in the reserves

The forest reserves around Tākaka and the Abel Tasman boundary support strong bird populations. Kākā, kererū, tūī, and bellbird are commonly seen. The Abel Tasman Birdsong project is actively restoring habitat and reintroducing species, so this is one of the easier ways to add native forest birdlife to a wider Golden Bay day.

Keep your distance

Seals are wild animals. Stay at least 20 meters away, do not approach, and never feed them. Mothers with pups are particularly sensitive to disturbance. The same applies to dolphins — observe from a distance and let them approach if they want to.

Practical tips

What actually helps.

Making the most of wildlife viewing in Golden Bay comes down to timing, distance, and knowing where to look. This works especially well when combined with a beach day, an Abel Tasman walk, or lunch back in Pōhara or Tākaka.

Quick rules

  • Seals are at Cape Farewell year-round — low tide gives the best view.
  • Dolphins are seasonal — summer (Dec-March) is most reliable.
  • Kayak tours offer the best dolphin encounter odds.
  • Bird reserves are best visited early morning.
  • Always keep 20m distance from seals and dolphins.

Ethical viewing

  • Do not feed any wildlife.
  • Stay on marked tracks at seal colonies.
  • Keep noise levels low.
  • Do not approach animals — let them come to you if they choose.
