About the Region

Where the mountains meet the sea

Pohara sits at the heart of Golden Bay, a sheltered inlet at the top of New Zealand's South Island. With its pale golden sand, calm sheltered waters, and a backdrop of towering beech forest and marble mountains, it's one of the country's most unspoiled coastal destinations.

Just minutes from the edge of Abel Tasman National Park, Pohara is the perfect base for exploring one of New Zealand's most beloved Great Rides — whether by kayak, on foot, or by scenic water taxi. After a day in the wild, the village offers warm hospitality, excellent local cafes, and some of the sunniest weather in the country.

Things to Do

Adventures for every mood

From relaxing on the beach to multi-day tramps — Pohara is your launchpad for Golden Bay exploration.

Beach & Swimming

Pohara's golden sand beach is sheltered and safe for swimming, paddleboarding, and kayaking — perfect for families and relaxation alike.

Abel Tasman Track

Access the Abel Tasman Coast Track from nearby — day walks, multi-day tramps, and stunning coastal scenery await in NZ's smallest national park.

Kayaking & Water Taxi

Paddle into remote coves and beaches by kayak, or hop on a water taxi to explore the national park's most secluded spots.

Fishing & Diving

Golden Bay offers excellent fishing — from the wharf or charter a boat. The clear waters also make for great snorkelling and diving.

Wine & Local Produce

Golden Bay is home to boutique vineyards, cideries, and farm gates. Sample the region's artisanal food and wine in stunning settings.

Nature & Wildlife

Spot seals at Cape Farewell, birdlife in the forest reserves, and dolphins from the coast. Nature is everywhere in Golden Bay.

Must-See

Golden Bay highlights

Some of New Zealand's most iconic landscapes and experiences are right on Pohara's doorstep.

Abel Tasman National Park

New Zealand's smallest national park is also its most accessible and beloved. Golden sand beaches, granite headlands, and crystal-clear tidal estuaries make every visit unforgettable. The Coast Track is a Great Ride — tackle it as a day walk or a multi-day adventure.

The Separation Point Walk

One of Golden Bay's finest short walks — a 2.5-hour return track through ancient beech forest to a stunning headland with views up and down the coast. Watch for the rare and beautiful kaka and tieke (saddleback) in the surrounding canopy.

Cape Farewell & Wharariki Beach

At the top of the South Island, Cape Farewell is where the Tasman Sea meets the Pacific Ocean. Wharariki Beach — a short walk from the road end — features towering sand dunes, archways, and seals at play in the surf.

Getting to Pohara

Pohara is an easy drive from Nelson and well signposted from the top of the South Island.

Location

Pohara, Golden Bay
South Island, New Zealand

By Car

2 hours from Nelson via SH60 through the Rai Valley. 45 min from Takaka. Well graded roads — 2WD accessible year-round.

By Air

Fly to Nelson Airport, then hire a car for the scenic 2-hour drive to Pohara.

By Ferry

The ferry from the North Island arrives in Picton — a scenic 3-hour drive south to Nelson, then up to Pohara.
