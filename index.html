About the Region

Coastal charm at the edge of Abel Tasman

Pohara is one of the best places to stay in Golden Bay if you want easy beach access, relaxed seaside accommodation, and quick day trips to Abel Tasman National Park. The village is known for its long sweep of golden sand, calm swimming water, and easy access to walks, cafes, galleries, and local food.

From a Pohara base, you can explore the Abel Tasman Coast Track, visit Te Waikoropupū Springs, swim at Ligar Bay and Tata Beach, and discover some of the best things to do in Golden Bay without spending your whole trip in the car. It is a practical, family-friendly base with real local character.

Start with the best beaches around Pohara, add an Abel Tasman day walk from the Golden Bay side, then round out the trip with wildlife stops, places to eat in Pohara and Tākaka, and a few local gallery and craft stops.

Must-See

Golden Bay highlights

Some of New Zealand's most iconic landscapes and experiences are right on Pohara's doorstep.

Taupo Point

Abel Tasman National Park

New Zealand's smallest national park is also its most accessible and beloved. Golden sand beaches, granite headlands, and crystal-clear tidal estuaries make every visit unforgettable. The Coast Track is one of New Zealand's best-loved Great Walk experiences, whether you tackle it as a day walk or a multi-day adventure.

Farewell Spit

Farewell Spit

Farewell Spit is a dramatic sweep of dunes, sand flats, and ever-changing light at the northern edge of Golden Bay. It is one of those places that feels vast and elemental, with coastal birdlife, open horizons, and a raw sense of space you do not forget quickly.

Wharariki Beach

Cape Farewell & Wharariki Beach

At the top of the South Island, Cape Farewell is where the Tasman Sea meets the Pacific Ocean. Wharariki Beach — a short walk from the road end — features towering sand dunes, archways, and seals at play in the surf.

Kaituna Track

Walking Tracks

Beyond the beaches, Golden Bay is full of beautiful bush walks, riverside tracks, and cool forest swimming spots. They are perfect for slower days, with easy options close to Pohara as well as longer walks deeper into native forest.

Te Waikoropupu Springs

Just inland from Takaka, these springs are some of the clearest in the country. A short walk through native forest leads to the main pool — icy cold but incredible to see. The springs are sacred to Māori and locally managed.

Pupu Springs

Wainui Falls

A short and rewarding walk from the road end at Wainui. The track winds through native bush before opening to this beautiful waterfall. Great for a refreshing swim in summer, or just enjoying the mist and forest atmosphere year-round.

Local Cafes & Restaurants

Fresh seafood, local produce, and warm country hospitality — Pohara and Takaka have an excellent range of eateries to suit all tastes and budgets. Enjoy fresh local flavours and welcoming atmospheres.

Things to Do

Adventures for every mood

From relaxing on the beach to day walks in Abel Tasman, Pohara is one of the easiest bases for the best things to do in Golden Bay.

Beach & Swimming

Pohara's golden sand beach is sheltered and safe for swimming, paddleboarding, and kayaking — perfect for families. Calm waters year-round.

Learn More

Abel Tasman Coast Track

One of New Zealand's best-loved coastal walks, with options from simple day walks to full multi-day tramps. Golden sand beaches, granite headlands, and tidal estuaries make it an easy yes from a Pohara base.

Learn More

Nature & Wildlife

Spot seals at Cape Farewell, birdlife in forest reserves, and dolphins from the coast. The Abel Tasman Birdsong project is restoring native bird populations.
